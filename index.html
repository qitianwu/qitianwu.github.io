Bio

I am a PhD candidate at the Department of Computer Science and Engineering from Shanghai Jiao Tong University (SJTU), advised by Prof. Junchi Yan. I also collaborated with David Wipf and Prof. Hongyuan Zha. I achieved the Bachelor (Microelectronics, Mathematics) and Master (Computer Science) degrees from SJTU, and worked as research intern at Tencent WeChat, Amazon AI Lab and BioMap.

My primary research area is machine learning. Currently I focus on learning with semi-structured data (graphs and sequences), with applications to scientific discovery and recommender systems. The goal of my research is to design and build theoretically principled and practically useful models and tools that possess desired expressivity, scalability and robustness for handling real data from heterogenous, open and uncertain environments.

I have published 10+ papers on ICLR, NeurIPS, ICML and a recipient of Baidu Scholarship (10 candidates worldwide each year) and Microsoft PhD Fellowship (11 candidates in Asia). I was also selected as one of the top 100 AI Chinese new stars. Here are some of my highlighted recent works:

  • Learning with Distribution Shifts: Out-of-distribution Generalization on Graphs (EERM, MoleOOD) and Sequences (Caseq), Out-of-Distribution Detection on Graphs (GraphDE)
  • Scalable Transformers/GNNs for Large Graphs: Node-Level Transformers with Linear Complexity (NodeFormer)
  • Learning from Limited Observations: Negative-Free Self-supervised GNNs (CCA-SSG), Weakly-supervised GNNs (WSGNN)
  • Open-World Entity Extrapolation: Inductive Collaborative Filtering IDCF, Feature Extrapolation Networks FATE

I am always open for collaborations and if you are interested in my research feel free to contact me via email or WeChat (id: myronwqt228).
